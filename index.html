🔬 Risk Methodologies

Value at Risk (VaR)

Maximum expected loss at a given confidence level (99%) over a holding period. Methods: Historical simulation · Monte Carlo · Parametric

Expected Shortfall (ES / CVaR)

Average loss beyond VaR — required under FRTB / Basel IV. More sensitive to tail risk than standard VaR.

Stress Testing

Re-pricing portfolio under historical scenarios (2008, COVID, 2020 oil crash) and hypothetical shocks.

Greeks (Options)

Delta, Gamma, Vega, Theta, Rho — first & second order sensitivities computed in real time across all listed and OTC derivatives.

Limit Monitoring

Hierarchy of risk limits (desk → book → trader) with automated alerts at 80% utilisation and hard stops at 100%.

🔄 Clearing Lifecycle

1️⃣
Trade Capture

Trade details submitted to CCP via clearing gateway (FpML / FIX)

2️⃣
Novation

Original contract replaced by two contracts — each party now faces the CCP

3️⃣
Margin Call

Initial Margin (IM) + Variation Margin (VM) calculated and called within minutes

4️⃣
Netting

Offsetting positions across members compressed — reduces gross exposure by up to 90%

5️⃣
Default Waterfall

Defaulting member's margin → their default fund contribution → CCP equity → mutualized fund

Major CCPs: CME Clearing LCH.Clearnet Eurex Clearing ICE Clear JSCC

Margin & Collateral Management
The financial buffers that protect the CCP and system from default losses

💰 Margin Types

Initial Margin (IM)

Deposited upfront to cover potential future exposure over the close-out period. Calculated using SPAN, TIMS, or SIMM (for OTC). Typically covers 3–5 days of price moves.

Variation Margin (VM)

Daily (or intraday) mark-to-market settlement of gains & losses. Loser pays winner each day, preventing loss accumulation.

Default Fund (Guarantee Fund)

Mutualized pool contributed by all clearing members. Used only after a defaulting member's IM is exhausted. Calculated via stress scenarios.

Eligible Collateral

Cash (USD, EUR, GBP…), G10 government bonds, agency bonds, equities (with haircuts). Concentrated collateral limits apply.

🏗️ Settlement Infrastructure

Central Securities Depositories (CSDs)

Hold legal title to securities in dematerialised form. Examples: DTCC/NSCC (US), Euroclear, Clearstream, JASDEC (JP)

RTGS — Real-Time Gross Settlement

Central bank systems (FedWire, TARGET2, CHAPS) settle cash leg in real time, one transaction at a time — no netting, no credit risk.

Delivery versus Payment (DvP)

Simultaneous atomic exchange of securities and cash — eliminates principal risk (Herstatt risk).

Securities Financing (Repo / Stock Borrow)

Repo & stock-lending markets provide the liquidity to cover settlement fails and short positions. Tri-party agents (BNY, JP Morgan) manage collateral.

Key Takeaways
What makes modern electronic trading, risk & clearing extraordinary
⚡

Speed & Scale

Matching engines execute in <1 µs. Global exchanges handle millions of orders per second. Co-location means latency is measured in nanoseconds.

🛡️

Layered Risk Controls

Pre-trade, intraday, and post-trade risk checks form an overlapping safety net. Circuit breakers, margin, and the CCP waterfall protect the whole system.

🏛️

CCP as Systemic Safeguard

Central clearing with novation, netting and margin eliminates bilateral credit risk. Post-2008 reforms mandated clearing of most OTC derivatives.

📐

DvP Eliminates Principal Risk

Simultaneous exchange of securities and cash in atomic transactions ensures you never pay without receiving — removing settlement failure risk.

📜

Regulatory Complexity

MiFID II, Dodd-Frank, EMIR, Basel IV — a dense web of global regulation drives transparency, reporting, and capital requirements across the entire chain.

🤖

Technology is the Moat

Low-latency infrastructure, AI-driven surveillance, and real-time risk engines are critical differentiators — and barriers to entry — in modern markets.
